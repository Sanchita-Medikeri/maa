01 · Childhood Innocence

The woman in the saree who made me brave

Then — the very beginning
"She never missed a single recital. She stood at the back so I could stand at the front."

In this photo, she is wearing her silk saree, her bangles, her kumkum — the version of herself she kept polished even on ordinary days. And behind me, she is smiling the way she always smiles. Quietly. Proudly. Like she knew something good was about to happen.

02 · Silent Sacrifices

She walked into the snow and smiled like it wasn't cold

✦

Look at this woman. Standing in the mountains, in the snow, laughing at cold boots and grey skies. She didn't wait for anyone to take her there. She went. Because that is who she is — not fragile, not small. Enormous.

"She let us go far — so we could become who we needed to be. She stayed back and she never once made us feel the weight of that."

That is the sacrifice that no one photographs. The quiet kind. The staying kind.

04 · Everyday Care

Love that sounds like
"Jevanlas ka re?"

The kind of love that doesn't make headlines. It shows up in WhatsApp messages at 8pm. In the food that's always somehow ready. In the "are you okay?" that comes before any hello.

🍱
Jevanlas ka?
Before anything else — before "how are you", before "what's the news" — she asks if I have eaten. Every day. Without fail.
📱
The daily check-in
A call. A missed call. A "reply when you can." She never says she's worried. She just calls again. And again. Until she hears my voice.
🌙
She stays up
When I travel. When I have exams. When I am quiet for too long. She doesn't sleep easy until she knows I am okay. I only noticed this later.
🤲
Silent prayers
She prays for me before I know I need it. I have never seen her skip this. Not once. Not ever.
05 · Emotional Realisation

Someone once said to me —
"You look a lot like your mother."

I have never been more proud of anything. Not a degree. Not an achievement. Not a title.

If I carry even a fraction of her inner strength — the kind that holds everything together without asking for credit — then I am doing something right.

✦

"She never fell apart in front of us.
She waited until we weren't looking."

06 · Gratitude

The four of us.
Because of her.

"Some mothers never ask to be appreciated. They simply continue — softly, fiercely, quietly — loving."

This family — this whole photograph — exists because she held it together. With both hands. On the hard days and the ordinary ones and the ones nobody remembers.
